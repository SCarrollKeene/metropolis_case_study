Metropolis Case Study

By Stephen Carroll-Keene

The Problem

Mel needs a coffee shop close to home where she can work from that provides a sense of safety, good atmosphere and community, but she is struggling to finding this through internet searches thus far

Timeline

Ongoing

Role

Myself and 2 other designers acted as UX designers, Front End Web Developer

Tools

Figma, Miro, Whimsical, Google Forms, Visual Studio Code, GitHub

Research

Affinity Diagram

Created from both user and stake interviews that we conducted and organized as a group, key insights included the focus on local community, online presence and the history of the coffee shop.

Coffee Survey

One of the first steps in our research process was to conduct a survey based on coffee. The survey ranged from how much coffee a user consumed to where they got there coffee and convenience in where they picked up their from.

User Insights

Interview data gathered from numerous participants that were used towards creating our user persona.

Definiton and Ideation

User Persona

Mel is a remote worker seeking a new shop to work from away from home that's safe, gives a sense of community and looks aesthitecally pleasing.

I Like, I Wish, What If Method

This served as a brainstorm of constructive feedback from a diverse group of users that helped in amassing a number ideas that could eventually be included within an app based on feasability.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

This matrix was used to gather feedback from a number of users on features and changes they would love to see implemented in their choice of coffee shop based on feseability.

Storyboard

A sequence of events representing the steps that Mel took towards finding a local coffee shop to work in that fit all of her standards.

Style Tile

In researching ideas for a pallette to use for the site redesign, we had noted that the employee ran instagram page for Metropolis Denver had such a pleasing aesthetic so we decided to incorporate that into the style tile.

Iteration

Paper Prototypes

These were initially created as a means of finding new inspiration for the creation of the eventual redesign of Metropolis Coffee's existing website

Mid-Fidelity Wireframe

Alongside sketching out the paper prototypes, I led the charge with invisioning the site layout for the mid-fidelity that was also used for most of our user testing.

High-Fidelity Wireframe

With some feedback from testing in mind, content in the style guide was implemented into the layout creating our initial high-fidelity layout.

Conclusion

Final Thoughts

  • - This is an ongoing work in progress with the stakeholder as he wants to pay to have the entire site redesigned
  • - Working with the owner of Metropolis played a huge role in the vision for the redesign
  • - Testing the mid/high fidelity with the owner for any changes he’d like to see in the redesign prototype
  • - Hotjar & Google Analytics integration for testing of user traversal and where they like to click (if applicable)
  • - Mobile/Tablet low/mid wireframes for testing and responsive web design implementation
  • - Building out all missing web pages
  • - More iterating and testing of Home and Menu pages
